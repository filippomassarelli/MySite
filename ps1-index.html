Frontend Course Material

  • CSS:
    Sections 4 and 5 (video content: 4h30mn) take us through best practices of styling HTML with external CSS.

My Background


In order to bring some colour to who I am and where I come from, here's a short summary of my childhood, studies, work experience, and sports life.


Cultural


I am Italian, born in Rome. My mom who is a painter and my dad who is a food security expert for sustainable development projects so I hopped from country to country a lot.

I lived in Uganda, Romania, Russia, Kenya, Nicaragua, Yemen, Honduras, and Chad before turning 14, when my parents moved to Botswana I went to Rome to finish Highschool. I was tough to change friends every couple of years but on the upside I am fluent in four languagues and had a lot of cool pets too.


Academical


After changing a few of elementary schools my parents soon discovered that the French Lycee is the only educational system present in almost every country across the globe. Thus I did the French Baccalaureat, with a major in Science and a specialisation in advanced mathematics.

I didn't know what to study at University so I chose Mechanical Engineering mainly because I've always found maths easy, and phisics interesting. Plus Engineering seemed like the most logical choice for undecided people as it closes less doors than other fields of study. The only choice I had clear was that I wanted to study in the UK. So I went to the University of Southampton as it had ranked first for mechanical Engineering in the Guardian, and because it's by the sea ... at least on the map! Finally I specialised with a Master in Aerospace Engineering, but mainly because I found it interesting rather than because I wanted to work in the field.

With time I realised that continuous learning is must for me, as I find real pleasure in developing new skills. After University I have taken courses of Digital Marketing, Web Design, Data Science, Machine Learning, and now Web Development.


Professional


Thanks to professor Peter Webber, I was introduced to New Venture planning - one of my only non engineering modules at University. My business plan on a rather random idea (a beautifully designed solar powered cover for iPhone that rewards you the more you charge it) won the Santander UK University Awards. This fuelled the entrepreneurial momentum and soon I found myself in the first John Lewis Accelerator, running my own start-up.

After failing to find a sustainable business model I moved to Paris, to kick-start Teleperformance's Innovation Team, focussing on Corporate Innovation and Corporate Venture Capital. Hungry for my next entrepreneurial journey I soon moved to Rome in order to join Nanga Mai, a sustainable fashion startup as co-founder. In less than two years we grew Nanga Mai across 3 continents and made a small name for ourselves within the niche of high quality linen apparel. London soon came calling again, and with the experience gathered within the eraly stage startup ecosystem I joined Seedrs, where I am at time of writing (almost two years after joining) working as Portfolio Manager.

It is hard to build your professional identity but with my next step I believe I found the door to a path that would perfectly marry my creative abilities, engineering mindset and business acumen as well as passion of continuous self development.


Athletic


Like many kids I started with swimming and then moved to team sports. For me it was basketball. After 7 years of basketball I wanted a change and became passionate about martial arts. Starting with Karate I then transitioned to Muay Thai reaching a semi-professional level at University. Knee surgery forced me to leave combat sports and ever since I have been having fun with gymnastics and powerlifting.

Undertaking some form physical activity on a daily basis has been a constant in my life, and will - I hope - remain as such indefinitely.
